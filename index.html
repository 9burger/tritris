How to Play

Controls

Use the arrow keys to move left and right. Press down to soft drop. Use Z and X to rotate.

Mechanics

The mechanics are very similar to NES Tetris. It uses the same DAS, entry delay, and level speed timings.
All of the pieces are made out of 3 connected right triangles. There is also a white, single triangle piece which can be used to fix holes in your stack. It will always come out 3 at a time. The single triangle is also special, lines will only be cleared once they have all been placed. This allows you to fill 3 lines and score a Tritris!

Phasing

One of the most unique mechanics of Tritris is phasing. A piece will always move to the next location as long as the next location is valid (there are no intersecting triangles). Scoring

A Tritris is scored!

In Tritris, clearing 4 lines at a time is not possible, so the highest score score value is gotten from 3 lines at a time, a Tritris. This can only be achieved by settings up 3 rows, each missing half a triangle, then placing a single triangle in each row. Once all triangles are placed, the rows clear and a Tritris is scored!
A triple is equivalent to a Tetris in NES Tetris, a double is equivalent to a triple, and a single is equivalent to a double.
